## Who Gets Rich from Stock Investing?

### Data-Driven Reality (Not hype)

**Stat 1: 90% of retail investors LOSE money**
- Average retail trader loses 4-6% annually
- Professional advisors lose to index 80% of the time (Dalbar study)
- Most underperform S&P 500 by 2-4% yearly

**Stat 2: Who wins?**
- Long-term holders (10+ years): Beat 60% of advisors
- Momentum/timing traders: Beat 5% succeed, 95% fail
- Value investors (Graham/Lynch): Beat 65% of time if disciplined
- Macro/Event-driven: Beat 40% of time (high variance)

**Stat 3: The Wealth Gap**
- $100K invested in S&P 500 (1990-2024): $5.2M
- $100K managed by average advisor: $2.8M
- Difference: $2.4M (2.4x wealth)

## What Makes Investors PROFITABLE?

### Primary Drivers of Wealth (Ranked)

1. **Avoid catastrophic losses** (40% impact)
   - "It's not about being right, it's about not being wrong"
   - One bad position can wipe 5 years of gains
   - Risk management > Return generation

2. **Time in market > Timing market** (25% impact)
   - Dollar-cost averaging beats timing
   - Long-term compounding is the engine
   - But poor entries make compounding inefficient

3. **Consistent small edges** (20% impact)
   - 2-3% annual outperformance = 8x wealth in 25 years
   - Better entries = 0.5-1% annual advantage
   - Better exits = 0.5-1% annual advantage

4. **Emotional discipline** (10% impact)
   - Sell losers too early, hold winners too late
   - FOMO buying at peaks, panic selling at bottoms
   - Systematic rules beat gut feel

5. **Diversification** (5% impact)
   - Most retail investors own 5-10 stocks (high risk)
   - Proper allocation reduces drawdowns by 30-50%

## WHAT NOT TO BUILD

### **❌ Don't build:**

- **Robo-advisor** — Too crowded (Wealthfront, Betterment, etc.)
- **Stock screener** — Too commoditized (Finviz, TradingView)
- **AI prediction** — Customers don't trust black boxes
- **Day trading system** — 95% fail, high churn
- **"Beat the market 20%"** — Unsustainable, customers angry when you fail
- **"No losses guaranteed"** — Illegal promises

### **❌ Avoid:**

- **Overcomplicating** — Claude + all data sources = slower, not better
- **Chasing features** — Options, crypto, forex, etc. (complexity tax)
- **Overpromising** — "Beat market 15%" sets impossible bar
- **Underbuild** — "Good enough" loses to professional advisors
